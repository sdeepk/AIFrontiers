2.5 Guardrails & Responsible AI
EU AI Act: Enforcement escalating. Mandatory oversight, conformity assessments, incident reporting.
Agents take actions: The risk profile is fundamentally different from chatbots.
Liability: AI hiring bias, hallucinated advice, deepfake fraud have all resulted in legal action.
The EU AI Act became law in August 2024 and enforcement is escalating through 2026 — high-risk AI systems now face mandatory human oversight, transparency requirements, conformity assessments, and incident reporting. Non-compliance penalties are material. Also mention the India DPDP Act, which has implications for data used in AI training, inference, and personalisation — especially relevant for India-based IT services handling client data. The key shift from chatbots to agents: an agent that writes to a database, sends an email, or executes a code change has real-world consequences. The era of "it's just a prototype" is over.
