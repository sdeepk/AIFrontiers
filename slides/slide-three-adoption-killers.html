The Three Adoption Killers

Fear of replacement — Address directly. AI makes individuals more valuable per hour.
First bad experience — One hallucination kills adoption for months. Start low-stakes.
Tool fragmentation — Different tools per developer = no compounding benefit.
Address fear of replacement directly and repeatedly: reframe it as AI making individuals more valuable per hour of work. The risk to someone who uses AI well is far lower than the risk to someone who refuses to learn it. On first bad experience: one hallucination that embarrasses someone in front of a client kills adoption in that team for months — this is why you start with lower-stakes use cases and build trust before deploying in high-visibility scenarios. On tool fragmentation: if every developer uses a different AI tool with no shared practices, you lose the compounding benefit entirely. Standardise tools and build shared context libraries.
