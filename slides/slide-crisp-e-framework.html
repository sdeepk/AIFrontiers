The CRISP-E Framework

C Context — Who are you, what system, what codebase? R Role — What expert role should AI play? I Intent — What exact outcome do you need? S Specifics — Constraints, stack, conventions, format P Prior art — Examples of good and bad outputs E Evaluation — How will you know if the output is correct?

The E (Evaluation) is the new addition. It matters most.

The E — Evaluation — is the 2026 addition and it matters most. When you tell the model how you will evaluate its output, it produces measurably better output. Saying "I will check this function by calling it with edge cases X, Y, and Z" in the prompt consistently improves code quality versus prompts without an evaluation criterion. This is not just a mnemonic — it is a mental checklist. Context and Prior art are the two most commonly skipped elements, and they make the biggest difference.
