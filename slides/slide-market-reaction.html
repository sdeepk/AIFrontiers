The Jevons Paradox: The market got it backwards
JPEG compression didn't reduce storage demand — it expanded what people stored
More efficiency → more adoption → more infrastructure spend
The Jevons Paradox is the key mental model here. JPEG compression did not reduce storage demand — it expanded what people stored. Making inference cheaper will expand the total volume of AI inference, not shrink hardware demand. More workloads become economically viable; total AI infrastructure spend goes up. History consistently shows that efficiency gains expand markets. The memory chip stock drop is a buying signal, not a sell signal — the addressable market for AI services grows as inference gets cheaper.
