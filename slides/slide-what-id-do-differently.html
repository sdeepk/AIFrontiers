What I'd Do Differently

Define ROI metrics BEFORE you build — make them binary (yes/no)
Invest in context architecture before model sophistication
Build evaluation infrastructure on day one
The first pilot should be something that ACTUALLY HURTS if it fails
These are hard-won lessons. On ROI metrics: make them binary — yes/no, not "somewhat better." On context architecture: most teams invest in model sophistication first, but the leverage is in the upstream context. On evaluation: building it as an afterthought means you ship without knowing if your system works. On the first pilot: toy problems do not generate organisational urgency — pick something that actually hurts if it fails, because that creates the executive sponsorship and attention needed to succeed. Transition: let us look at where Broadcom fits in this AI landscape.
