GraphRAG: When You Need It

What it unlocks: Relationship-heavy queries. Multi-hop reasoning over entity-relationship knowledge.
Cost caveat: 3–5× more LLM calls than baseline RAG
Decision rule: Use for relationship-heavy domains. Use vector + hybrid for most others.
Stack: Microsoft GraphRAG (open source) + Neo4j is the most production-mature.
GraphRAG builds an entity-relationship graph — people, products, policies, contracts as nodes, relationships as edges — instead of treating knowledge as a flat document collection. It enables queries like "What are all the compliance risks across our vendor contracts with EU suppliers?" — a multi-hop question that pure vector search cannot answer. Microsoft's GraphRAG is the reference implementation; Neo4j plus LLM is the most production-mature stack. But the cost caveat is real: 3-5x more LLM calls than baseline RAG for the extraction phase. Use it for relationship-heavy domains; vector plus hybrid search handles most others.
