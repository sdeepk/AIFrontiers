Where Projects Succeed: Discovery

Stakeholder alignment on the problem before discussing the solution
Executive sponsor with budget AND belief AND willingness to absorb early failure
Success metric everyone agrees is real and measurable before project starts
The most important point: align on the problem before discussing the solution. Too many discovery phases start with "we want to use AI" instead of "we have this painful problem." The executive sponsor needs budget AND belief AND willingness to absorb early failure — all three. A success metric must be real, measurable, and agreed by everyone before the project starts. If you cannot define success in binary terms, you are not ready for a POC.
