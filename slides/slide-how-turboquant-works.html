How TurboQuant Works

Stage 1: PolarQuant

Converts data vectors to polar coordinates after random rotation. Makes data distribution highly predictable. Eliminates per-block normalisation constants.

Stage 2: QJL

Quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss. A 1-bit error correction layer that eliminates residual bias. Total error approaches the information-theoretic lower bound.

The two stages are complementary. PolarQuant's key insight is that random rotation makes the data distribution highly predictable, which means you no longer need to store per-block normalisation constants — a major source of overhead. QJL stands for Quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss — it brings total error to near the information-theoretic lower bound, meaning you mathematically cannot compress much further without losing information. The combination achieves 6x reduction in KV cache memory footprint. Validated on Llama 3.1 and Gemini/Mistral model families.
