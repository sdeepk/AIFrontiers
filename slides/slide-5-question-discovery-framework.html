The 5-Question Discovery Framework

Audience Exercise

What specific decision or task is currently manual, slow, or error-prone?
What data is available, where does it live, and who owns access?
Who will use this system and what does their current workflow look like?
What does success look like in 6 months — in measurable, binary terms?
Who will own and evaluate this system after it's built?
Use this live with the audience — ask someone to volunteer a real use case and walk through the five questions. The power of this framework is that it forces clarity before any technology decision. Question 4 is the hardest: "What does success look like in 6 months in measurable, binary terms?" Most teams cannot answer this, and that is the signal that they are not ready for a POC. Question 5 catches the orphan problem: if nobody will own and evaluate the system after it is built, it will degrade and be abandoned. This closes Part 1 — transition to the break.
