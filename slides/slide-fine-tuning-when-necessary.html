Fine-Tuning: When It's Actually Necessary

Step 1: Can a well-engineered context window solve this? 1M tokens + structured retrieval → try this first.
Step 2: Can Agentic RAG with GraphRAG solve this? Try this before fine-tuning.
Step 3: Is the problem about proprietary taxonomy? → Fine-tuning helps.
Step 4: Do you have 1,000+ high-quality labelled examples? If no: collect data first.
Step 5: Consider fine-tuning.
The case for fine-tuning has narrowed, not expanded, as frontier models improved and long-context capabilities grew. Default to context engineering and agentic RAG first — it is cheaper, faster, and does not require data labelling. When fine-tuning is justified, LoRA/QLoRA is still the gold standard — roughly 10x cheaper than full fine-tuning; you can run a 70B model on a single A100 with QLoRA. RLAIF (RL from AI Feedback) reduces human labelling burden by 60-80%. For many apparent fine-tuning use cases, cached system prompts with rich few-shot examples are sufficient and far cheaper.
