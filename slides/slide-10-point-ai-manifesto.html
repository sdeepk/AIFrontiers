The AI Manifesto

Context is the craft. The quality of your AI output is determined by what you give it.
AI is a collaborator, not an oracle. You own every line, every decision, every outcome.
Evaluate everything you ship. Without evaluation, you ship without tests.
Spec before you code. Define precisely — AI helps you build dramatically better.
Review everything. AI is confident even when wrong. You are the last line of defence.
Route intelligently. Right model for the right task.
Security is non-negotiable. Never send sensitive data without policy clearance.
Share what works. Prompt libraries, MCP connectors, evaluation datasets — hoard nothing.
Learn from every interaction. The developer who learns faster survives every shift.
Build responsibly. Every AI system you ship shapes someone's experience. Make it worth trusting.
Read these slowly — let each one land. These ten points summarize everything from the session into principles you can internalize. Emphasize points 1, 3, and 8 especially: context is the craft (not prompting), evaluate everything you ship (the biggest gap), and share what works (prompt libraries and evaluation datasets are team assets that compound — hoard nothing). Consider printing this and posting it where your team works. These are not aspirational statements; they are daily operating principles.
