What Actually Works: Building AI Culture

Champions program: 3–5 early adopters per team. Peer influence beats mandates.
Live demos with real data: Show AI on their actual codebase, not generic demos.
AI office hours: Weekly sessions to experiment safely and share discoveries.
Shared prompt libraries: Internal repo of prompts for your stack. This is team IP.
Peer influence is more effective than top-down mandates — the champions program works because people trust their teammates' recommendations more than management directives. For live demos, the key is using their actual codebase, their actual documents, their actual workflows — generic AI demos are unconvincing. AI office hours should be judgment-free zones where people can experiment safely. The shared prompt library is an internal GitHub repo of prompts that work well for your stack, your domain, your coding conventions — treat it as team IP that compounds over time.
